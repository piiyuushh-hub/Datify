Machine Learning Architecture

We evaluated three distinct regression algorithms to accurately map customer demographics and behavioral habits to their monthly data usage.

🏆 SELECTED PRODUCTION MODEL
Random Forest Regressor
R² Score: ~0.97
An ensemble learning method that constructs a multitude of decision trees at training time. It operates by outputting the mean prediction of the individual trees, making it highly robust against overfitting while capturing complex, non-linear patterns in user behavior.

Why we chose this model

Data usage patterns are highly non-linear. For example, streaming hours heavily dictate data usage, but the impact changes drastically depending on the Wi-Fi ratio. Random Forest easily models these complex feature interactions without requiring extensive manual feature engineering. It achieved the highest accuracy across our cross-validation sets.

Gradient Boosting Regressor
R² Score: ~0.95
A sequential ensemble method that builds trees one at a time, where each new tree helps to correct errors made by the previously trained tree. While extremely powerful, it can be slightly more prone to overfitting on noisy data compared to Random Forest.
Trees (n_estimators) 30

Multiple Linear Regression
R² Score: ~0.72
A foundational statistical method that models the relationship between the independent variables (demographics, habits) and the dependent variable (data usage) by fitting a linear equation.
